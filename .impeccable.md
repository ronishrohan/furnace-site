## Design Context

### Users
Developers evaluating or using Furnace as a terminal-first coding harness. They visit to understand the product quickly, install it, learn its workflows, and track what changed between releases.

### Brand Personality
Open, exacting, and unconventional. The interface should feel handmade and technical without becoming cold, corporate, or generically futuristic.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve Furnace’s textured industrial surface, restrained day/night palette, pixel mark, Departure Mono chrome, and editorial Libre Baskerville moments. New pages should feel native to the existing site rather than introducing a separate visual system.

### Design Principles
- Make source evidence and product behavior more prominent than marketing language.
- Use typography, rules, and spacing for hierarchy instead of stacks of generic cards.
- Keep controls keyboard-accessible, responsive, and understandable without color alone.
- Preserve the site’s unusual material texture while keeping long-form content readable.
- Respect reduced motion and avoid decorative interaction that obscures information.
